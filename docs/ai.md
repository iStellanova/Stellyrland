# A Note on AI
## AI Today
I undestand that AI is an extremely controversial subject in today's world, but it isn't going away anytime soon. It has fundamentally changed the way many industries work, for better or worse. One of those industries is software development.

## Where it Belongs, at least IMO
AI shouldn't be the original author or tool in a product to be disributed. The user should handle the architecturing, decision-making, permissions, management, and in-depth analysis of the present project. Never should AI be the originator in something to be distributed. The safest way to use AI is in debugging and auditing.

## How do I use AI?
I do leverage AI tools in my workflow. I never ship software using it to do my job for me. Personal projects I can get lazy, espectially with repetitive boilerplate code or commit writing. In general, I mainly use AI to **audit, debug, explain, and understand** my code, as nix in particular can be a very difficult language. 

The future seems to be moving on with AI, and fighting and/or refusing it in any capacity at all makes me feel like I would be left behind. There is a place for it, as it is a tool like any other addition to a workflow. Use it wisely, and maybe I can leverage it to bring greater quality to my work I could otherwise never reach on my own at breakneck speed.
